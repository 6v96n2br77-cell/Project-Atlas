Atlas v1.0 Development

Creating the Competitive Market Model

The initial version of Atlas focused on creating a functioning competitive smartphone market simulation. Three firms were introduced: Nova, Vertex and Pulse.

Consumers were modelled using utility calculations based on three key factors: price, quality and brand reputation. Consumer preferences were randomised to represent heterogeneous demand.

Introducing Firm Strategy

Firms were given the ability to respond to market outcomes. Instead of remaining static, firms could alter characteristics such as price, quality and branding depending on performance.

This created more realistic competition, as firms began pursuing different strategies rather than simply competing on one factor.

Atlas v2.0 Development

Consumer Income Segmentation

A major improvement was introducing differences between consumers based on income. Previously, all consumers valued price, quality and brand equally.

Consumers now have different purchasing priorities. Lower-income consumers place greater emphasis on price, while higher-income consumers value quality and brand reputation more heavily.

Macroeconomic Conditions

The simulation was expanded beyond individual consumers and firms by introducing economic cycles.

Booms, normal periods and recessions now affect the size of the consumer market, creating different environments for firms to operate within.

Data Analysis and Visualisation

Graphing was introduced to analyse firm performance over time. Profit, revenue, costs and market share can now be tracked across multiple years.

This allowed the simulation results to be analysed rather than simply generated.

Current Focus

Atlas is currently being developed towards a more complete economic simulation, with future versions planned to incorporate additional macroeconomic systems.
