Atlas v1.0 Development

Creating the Competitive Market Model

The initial version of Atlas focused on creating a functioning competitive smartphone market simulation. Three firms were introduced being "Nova", "Vertex" and "Pulse".

Consumers were modelled using utility calculations based on three key factors: price, quality and brand reputation. Consumer preferences were randomised to represent heterogeneous demand.

Introducing Firm Strategy

Firms were given the ability to respond to market outcomes. Instead of remaining static, firms could alter characteristics such as price, quality and branding depending on performance.

This created more realistic competition, as firms began pursuing different strategies rather than simply competing on one factor.

Atlas v2.0 Development

Consumer Income Segmentation

A major improvement was introducing unique differences between consumers based on income. Previously, all consumers valued price, quality and brand equally.

Consumers now have different purchasing priorities. Lower-income consumers place greater emphasis on price, while higher-income consumers value quality and brand reputation more heavily.

Macroeconomic Conditions

The simulation was expanded beyond individual consumers and firms by introducing economic cycles.

Booms, normal periods and recessions now affect the size of the consumer market, creating different environments for firms to operate within.

Data Analysis and Visualisation

Graphing was introduced to analyse firm performance over time. Profit, revenue, costs and market share can now be tracked across multiple years.

This allowed the simulation results to be analysed rather than simply generated.

Atlas v3.0 Development

Markov Transition Model

The first implementation to v3.0 was a Markov Transition Cycle which uses realistic probabilities that an economy will cycle through economic stages to simulate natural economic expansions and contractions.

This addition is monumental for progression of Atlas to make the transition from a rough simulation to an accurate simulation with more precise detailing of stages owing to the inclusion of: Booms, Expansions, Stable periods, Slowdowns and Recessions.

Macroeconomic Functions

A grand total of eleven functions were introduced and calibrated to ensure Atlas could withold the genuine characteristics of a developed economy.

These functions allowed the calculation of RGDP, inflation, interest rates, consumer confidence, borrowing propensity etc. which have been calculated to ensure unrealistic fluctuations (Such as -50% RGDP) are not present in the simulation.

Consumer Base

Consumers now have budgets which are deciphered based on their income level as to prevent the outcome that low-income consumers are purchasing premium priced products. In addition to this, consumers now have the ability to finance the smartphones as income should not be the sole determinant of their decisions.

Production Cost Adaptations

As smartphones carry rising marginal costs, it was important to the project that this could be refelected therefore costs-per-unit now rise in accordance with this ideology.

This shows how Atlas recognises the idea of supply constraints, a very much true issue for real-world industries.

Current Focus

Atlas is currently being developed towards a more complete economic simulation, with future versions planned to incorporate broader macroeconomic systems involing unemployment, government intervention, productivity levels etc. in addition to more intricate industrial systems.
