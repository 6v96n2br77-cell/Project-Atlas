What is Atlas?

Project Atlas is an agent-based economic simulation developed in Python. The project models interactions between consumers, firms and economic conditions to explore how markets evolve over time.

Rather than creating a fixed economic model, Atlas focuses on dynamic interactions where individual decisions combine to create wider market outcomes.

Project Motivation

Atlas was created to combine interests in economics, programming and computational modelling.

Economic theory often relies on simplified assumptions about consumer behaviour and firm decision-making. Atlas explores how these assumptions can be translated into a working simulation and gradually improved through iterative development.

Development Approach

Atlas has been developed through multiple versions, with each version introducing new systems and addressing limitations discovered in previous models.

  • Atlas v1.0 established the competitive market framework.
  • Atlas v2.0 introduced consumer segmentation and macroeconomic conditions.
  • Future versions aim to incorporate increasingly complex economic systems.

Atlas is an ongoing project. Development continues through additional versions, with the aim of creating a more realistic representation of economic behaviour and market dynamics.
