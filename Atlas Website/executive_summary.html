Project Overview

Project Atlas is an agent-based economic simulation developed in Python. The project models interactions between consumers, firms and macroeconomic conditions to investigate how competitive markets evolve over time.

The simulation focuses on a three-firm smartphone market consisting of Nova, Vertex and Pulse. Each firm competes through different strategic approaches involving price, quality and brand positioning.

Objective

The primary objective of Atlas is to translate economic theory into a dynamic computational model. Rather than assuming identical consumers and static firms, Atlas introduces heterogeneous consumer preferences and adaptive business strategies.

The project aims to explore how individual consumer decisions aggregate into wider market outcomes, including profitability, revenue generation, market share and competitive behaviour.

Simulation Structure

  • The status of the macroeconomy is calculated in accordance with the Markov Transition Model.
  • Consumers are assigned individual income levels and preference weights relative to the macroeconomy.
  • Income, borrowing propensity and confidence influences the importance consumers place on price, quality and brand.
  • Consumers select firms based on calculated utility values.
  • Firms respond to performance changes through strategic adjustments.
  • Macroeconomic conditions influence market size and demand.

Current Capabilities

  • Agent-based consumer modelling
  • Firm competition and adaptive strategies
  • Income-based market segmentation
  • Economic boom, expansion, stable period, slowdown and recession cycles
  • Financial performance analysis
  • Data visualisation through Python

Key Findings

Atlas demonstrates that firms can achieve different competitive outcomes depending on their strategic positioning.

  • Nova achieves high profitability through premium positioning and stronger margins.
  • Vertex achieves high revenue through larger sales volume and market presence.
  • Pulse maintains market penetration by targeting price-sensitive consumers.

Future Direction

Future versions of Atlas will expand the economic environment by introducing additional macroeconomic systems, including inflation, interest rates, unemployment and economic shocks.

The long-term objective is to develop an increasingly realistic agent-based economic simulation capable of modelling complex interactions between firms, consumers and wider economic conditions.
