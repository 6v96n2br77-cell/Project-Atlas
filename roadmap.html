Atlas v3.0 - Macroeconomic Expansion

The next stage of Atlas development will focus on introducing broader macroeconomic systems and increasing realism within the simulated economy.

Current Features

  • Inflation affecting consumer purchasing power and firm costs
  • Interest rates influencing consumer decisions
  • Economic shocks and unexpected events
  • Supply Chain Constraints
  • Complex firm decision-making systems
  • Graphical interpretation of macroeconomic variables

Atlas v4.0 - Market Complexity

Future versions will focus on increasing the complexity of interactions between firms, consumers and the wider economy.

Potential Features

  • Research and development investment
  • Competition between industries
  • More advanced consumer behaviour

Atlas v5.0 - Financial and Economic Systems

Later versions may introduce financial systems to better represent the relationship between businesses, consumers and capital markets.

Potential Features

  • Bank lending and borrowing
  • Firm debt decisions
  • Investment cycles
  • Financial market influences

Long-Term Objective

The long-term goal of Atlas is to create an increasingly realistic agent-based economic simulation where firms, consumers and macroeconomic conditions interact dynamically over time.
